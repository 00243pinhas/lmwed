# SKILL: Augen Pro Reference
## Read this before designing or building any page section.
## Visit augen.pro and study it before every session.

## What Augen Pro Does That We Copy Directly

### 1. Numbered Navigation
```
01 Collections   02 Process   [LOGO]   03 About   04 Inquire →
```
- Numbers are part of the link — `01 Collections` not just `Collections`
- They signal structure and seriousness
- The `→` on the final CTA is the only directional hint in the nav

### 2. Section Markers
Every section opens with a small marker — top-left, always:
```
0.1 The Atelier
0.2 The Collection
1.0 Love Notes
2.0 Begin
```
- Format: Jost 10px uppercase, letter-spacing 0.16em, color --accent (#C4B8AC)
- Numbering logic: decimals within a page section, whole numbers for major sections
- Never centered — always left-aligned

### 3. Oversized Sparse Headlines
- Maximum 5–6 words per line
- Weight 300 (Light) — never bold
- Huge size (80–120px on desktop) with generous white space around it
- The space around the headline IS the design — do not fill it

### 4. Full-Bleed Dark Sections Alternating with Light
Page rhythm: dark hero → light content → dark pull → light content → dark CTA
Never two dark sections back to back without a light section between them.

### 5. Text-Only CTAs
```
View all looks →
Begin your journey →
See the full process →
```
- No buttons (except the inquiry submit)
- Text with hairline underline animation: slides left to right on hover, 300ms
- Jost 11px uppercase, letter-spacing 0.10em
- The `→` is always present — subtle direction signal

### 6. Extreme Negative Space
If a section feels too empty — it is probably right.
Augen Pro uses white space as a design element, not a gap to fill.
Minimum section padding: 80px mobile, 160px desktop.

### 7. Editorial Photography Approach
- Images are dark, dramatic, editorial
- Not bright, cheerful, or commercial
- A gradient overlay always darkens the bottom third
- Text sits on the darkened area — never on the bright part of an image

### 8. Oversized Step Numbers as Decoration
On process / numbered content pages:
- Numbers at 120px desktop / 80px mobile
- Color: very light (#E8E2DA) — structural, barely visible
- They are background elements, not readable numerals
- Content sits beside or over them

## What We Adapt for Bridal

| Augen Pro | LM Weddyli Adaptation |
|---|---|
| Cold, technical aesthetic | Warm restraint — still minimal but with human warmth |
| Product photography (tech) | Dress photography — dark editorial, not bright commercial |
| Sans-serif only | Serif (Cormorant) for display + sans (Jost) for UI |
| Very short copy | Slightly longer — brides need more trust-building than tech buyers |
| No testimonials | Testimonials woven in — trust is critical for remote purchase |
| Single product focus | Collection of 12 looks + a form journey |

---

## The Test
Before finalizing any section, ask:
*"If I removed the dress photography and put a tech product here, would this look like it belongs on augen.pro?"*

If yes — the design is right.
If no — it has too much bridal decoration. Strip it back.

## What Augen Pro Never Does (and Neither Do We)
- ❌ Rounded corners
- ❌ Drop shadows
- ❌ Decorative borders or ornamental dividers
- ❌ Icons or illustration in the UI
- ❌ Carousels with arrows
- ❌ Hover tooltips
- ❌ Gradient text
- ❌ Multiple typefaces (we use 2 — that's already the maximum)
- ❌ Loud color accents
- ❌ Centered headings (left-aligned or full-bleed only)
